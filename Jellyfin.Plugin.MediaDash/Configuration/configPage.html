shieldSafety

Guardrails that keep every scan and fix reversible.
On (default): fix runs only log what they would do — nothing is changed. Turn off once you're happy with the plan shown in History.
On (default): scheduled scans and fixes wait until nobody is watching anything and nobody has used Jellyfin for 15 minutes. The "Scan now" and "Run fixes now" buttons always run immediately.

tuneAdvanced

Fine-tuning and maintenance actions. Defaults are safe for most libraries.
Speed vs. quality trade-off for software re-encodes (libx264 / libx265 / libsvtav1). Hardware encoders (NVENC / AMF / QSV) use the plugin's bitrate ceiling instead — they don't support CRF.
Megabytes. Filters out sample files, trailers, and extras that shouldn't be flagged for re-encoding. Default: 100 MB.
HDR files (HDR10 / HDR10+ / HLG) are left alone by the quality scanner. Re-encoding HDR without proper color-space handling destroys HDR metadata and produces washed-out SDR, so opting in should be deliberate. Default: on.
Test-plays the start, middle and end of every file (30 seconds each). Catches broken files that look fine on the surface. The first scan is much slower; results are remembered for unchanged files. Default: on.
Off (default): a Director's Cut and the theatrical cut are kept as separate movies.
Movies become Name (Year) - {height}p.{ext}; TV episodes become SeriesName - S{ss:00}E{ee:00} - {height}p.{ext}. If Jellyfin doesn't have enough metadata for the file, the re-encode keeps its original basename. Default: off.
